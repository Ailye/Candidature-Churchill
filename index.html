My Timeline

  • Autumn 1888
    At Harrow, Winston was showing the advantages of his prodigious memory and his love for history and literature. He entered a competition with boys older than he (some were 17) which required him to “learn and work up the notes in Merchant of Venice, Henry VIII and Midsummer Night’s Dream.” He finished fourth out of 25.

    Churchill in the center (at the age of 14).
  • Autumn 1893
    Sandhurst Victories: Winston entered the Royal Military College at Sandhurst as a cavalry cadet because he had not qualified for the infantry, a circumstance of great disappointment to his father. He would soon be granted an opening in the infantry, but shortly after he entered school he was invited to attend a mess of the Fourth Queen’s Own Hussars, a light cavalry regiment.

    Churchill holding cavalery cadet.
  • Winter 1907-1908
    King Edward and Herbert Asquith, planning the latter’s succession to ailing Campbell-Bannerman as Prime Minister, heard gossip that WSC wanted to enter the Cabinet keeping his post of Under-Secretary. On 12 March Asquith offered WSC either the Colonial Office, the Local Government Board or the Admiralty; WSC asked for the Colonial Office, but on 8 April the new Prime Minister chose to offer his young colleague the Board of Trade.

    Winston Churchill.
  • 1926
    Churchill is surprised to learn that it is named Minister of Finance of the United Kingdom. At this station, Churchill will direct the “disastrous return to the Gold Standard which leads to deflation, the unemployment and the strike of the minors, first steps of the general strike of 1926” W. Manchester notes that it is despite everything “far from being the worst Chancellor of the Exchequer than knew the country”.

    Churchill Minister of Finance
  • 1940
    When he became Prime Minister during the Second World War, Winston Churchill formed a war cabinet. Churchill strongly believed that the War Cabinet should be kept to a relatively small number of individuals to allow efficient execution of the war effort. As the War Cabinet considered issues that pertained to a given branch of the service or government due input was obtained from the respective body.

    Churchill going in the War Cabinet
  • 1939-1945
    With the assistance of Antony Eden, it developed a profitable collaboration with president Franklin D. Roosevelt, obtaining the military and moral support of the United States. After the entry in war of the Soviet Union and the United States, in 1941, Churchill wove close links with the persons in charge of what it called “Great Alliance”, including with the general de Gaulle. Moving throughout all war, it contributed on the whole with the coordination of the allied military strategy. He played a leading role in the great conferences of peace, in particular in Yalta (1945).
